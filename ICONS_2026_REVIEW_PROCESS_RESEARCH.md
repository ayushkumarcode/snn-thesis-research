# ICONS 2026 Review Process and Conference Intelligence Report

**Research Date:** 15 March 2026
**Subject:** ACM International Conference on Neuromorphic Systems (ICONS) 2026
**Conference Dates:** August 4-6, 2026
**Venue:** Chicago Marriott Naperville, Chicago, Illinois

---

## Executive Summary

ICONS is a small-to-medium, specialist, peer-reviewed ACM conference focused exclusively on neuromorphic computing. It has grown from ~134 attendees in 2019 to 234+ by 2020, and from ~15 proceedings papers per year (2019-2023) to 85+ presentations at ICONS 2024. The only confirmed acceptance rate is ICONS 2023: **13 accepted from 22 submissions (59%)**, but this counts only proceedings papers -- the conference also accepts many more presentations as lightning talks and posters. The review process includes a **rebuttal phase** (confirmed for 2026). Papers not accepted for talks are considered for poster presentation. Best paper awards are given annually. The quality bar is approachable for well-executed student work -- this is not a top-tier mega-conference like NeurIPS but rather a focused community venue where national labs, mid-tier universities, and PhD students regularly publish.

## 1. Is It Peer-Reviewed?

**YES -- ICONS is a peer-reviewed ACM conference.**

### Evidence
- Submissions go through EasyChair (https://easychair.org/conferences?conf=icons26)
- Explicit review timeline: submissions April 1 -> reviews due May 13 -> reviews to authors May 18 -> rebuttal May 25 -> final decision June 5
- Published in ACM proceedings (2018-2023 via ACM DL; 2024 via IEEE/ACM co-publication)
- ACM sponsorship requires peer review per ACM Publications Policy

### Blind Review Type
**Not explicitly stated by ICONS.** The conference CFP does not specify single-blind or double-blind. However:
- ACM does not mandate a specific blind review type -- it is left to conference discretion
- For smaller ACM conferences using EasyChair, **single-blind review is the norm** (reviewers know authors, authors do not know reviewers)
- The neuromorphic community is small enough that double-blind would be largely ineffective
- **Best estimate: single-blind review**

### Number of Reviewers
**Not explicitly stated.** Based on:
- ACM standard practice for conferences: typically 2-3 reviewers per paper
- EasyChair default configuration: usually 3 reviewers
- For a conference of this size (~20-50 submissions for proceedings), 2-3 reviewers is standard
- **Best estimate: 2-3 reviewers per paper**

## 4. Review Criteria

### ICONS-Specific Criteria
ICONS does not publicly disclose its specific review criteria or scoring rubric. However, based on:

1. **ACM standard review criteria** (from ACM Publications Policy):
   - Originality/novelty of contribution
   - Technical correctness/soundness
   - Significance/importance to the field
   - Clarity of exposition and presentation quality
   - Relevance to the conference scope
   - Completeness of evaluation/experiments

2. **Typical EasyChair review form** (ACM conferences):
   - **Novelty/Originality** (1-5 scale): How novel is the contribution?
   - **Technical Soundness** (1-5 scale): Are methods correct and well-justified?
   - **Presentation Quality** (1-5 scale): Is the paper well-written and organized?
   - **Relevance** (1-5 scale): Is the work relevant to neuromorphic systems?
   - **Significance** (1-5 scale): How important is the contribution?
   - **Overall Recommendation**: Strong accept / Accept / Weak accept / Borderline / Weak reject / Reject / Strong reject
   - **Confidence**: Reviewer's self-assessed expertise level
   - **Detailed Comments**: Written feedback for authors

3. **ICONS-specific focus areas** (from CFP):
   - Neuromorphic circuits, sensors, and non-von Neumann architectures
   - Event/spike-based systems and emerging hardware implementations
   - Training algorithms (supervised, unsupervised, self-supervised, continual learning)
   - Energy-efficient AI and biomedical applications
   - Neuromorphic software, simulators, compilers, and visualization tools

## 5. Rebuttal/Response Phase

**YES -- ICONS 2026 has a formal rebuttal phase.**

- Reviews sent to authors: **May 18, 2026**
- Rebuttal deadline: **May 25, 2026** (7 days to respond)
- Rebuttal is sent to **program co-chairs** (not directly to reviewers)
- Final decision after rebuttal: **June 5, 2026** (11 days for deliberation)

This is a relatively recent addition. ICONS 2022 and 2023 did NOT have a rebuttal phase (notification came immediately after reviews). The rebuttal was introduced for ICONS 2025/2026.

**Rebuttal strategy implications:**
- 7 days is short -- prepare template responses in advance
- Rebuttals go to PC chairs, not individual reviewers -- focus on factual corrections and addressing key concerns
- The 11-day deliberation period after rebuttal suggests chairs integrate rebuttal into their decision-making

## 7. What Happens If Rejected?

### Explicit Policy from ICONS 2026 CFP

The ICONS 2026 CFP explicitly states:

> "If accepted, full papers will be included in the conference proceedings and considered for full talks (20 minutes). **Non-accepted papers will be considered for poster presentations.**"

> "If accepted, short papers will be included in the conference proceedings and considered for short talks (10 minutes). **Non-accepted papers will be considered for poster presentations.**"

**This means:**
- Rejected proceedings papers can still be presented as **posters**
- Poster-only presentations are NOT included in the proceedings
- This is a soft landing -- your work can still be presented at the conference even if not accepted as a proceedings paper

### Practical Implication
For your SNN-ESC50 paper: even in the worst case of proceedings rejection, you would likely still get to present a poster at ICONS 2026, assuming the work is relevant and technically sound.

- More focused on neuromorphic/hardware -- your SpiNNaker work is central here

1. Neuromorphic systems is its core focus (unlike IJCNN which is very broad)

3. The community values energy analysis and encoding studies
